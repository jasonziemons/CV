Welcome to my online CV!

I am Jason Ziemons, a recent graduate in Electrical Engineering with an ardent passion for software development and a deep fascination for microcontroller programming. My journey in the realm of engineering has been propelled by a drive to innovate, automate, and create streamlined solutions wherever I can. My foundation as an Electrical Engineer, coupled with my fervor for software development, has equipped me with a dynamic skill set. My journey has been enriched by a variety of experiences, including my successful time in a IT role where I excelled as a Salesforce Administrator. This unique role not only honed my interpersonal abilities but also ingrained in me a solid grasp of technology.

I am now driven to transition into an engineering role, combining my technological skills with my passion for problem-solving, design, and my desire to constantly keep learning for the next challenge. The exhilarating prospect of contributing to the world of engineering and software by developing my own solutions has fueled my journey with an unwavering sense of purpose.

Join me as I lead you through my journey, experiences, and projects, each representing a significant milestone in my pursuit of knowledge. Career Objective

Ultimately, my goal is to move into a software based role and thrive in an environment that encourages professional development, values creativity, and empowers me to make meaningful contributions to the engineering industry. I am excited to embark on this journey, and I am eager to find a role where I can embrace new challenges, learn continuously, and contribute to shaping the future of technology.

2021 - 2022

Honours Project

My honours project took the software route of my degree where we were tasked with using machine learning methods to identify and classify depictions of lighning in 18th century paintings. The goal was to determine if Benjamin Franklins presence had an impact on art culture during his time spent in France. In this project we trained and modified a Convolutional Neural Network (CNN) in Matlab to achieve our goal. A variant of this paper was published on IEEE and was showcased at the 2022 International Conference on Lightning Protection (ICLP) in Cape Town.
